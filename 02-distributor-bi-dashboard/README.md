# Distributor BI Dashboard

*A national distributor group operating fast-moving consumer goods, inventory, and retail distribution across multiple channels.*

---

## The problem

The distributor lacked a single source of truth for inventory health, lead times, order fill rates, SKU profitability, buyer behavior, and category-level performance. Sales and operations teams were making decisions from fragmented reports rather than a consolidated BI dashboard.

## The data

Four tables, 18,420 rows total. Distributor orders, inventory snapshots, sales transactions, channel allocations, and product master data. Analytics built for dashboard storytelling with KPI-driven insights.

## The three documents

1. **Executive Summary** — concise business recommendations for leadership. 8 pages.
2. **Dashboard Methodology Report** — design approach, data sources, metric definitions, dashboard logic. 12 pages.
3. **Dashboard Findings Report** — insights, scorecards, operational recommendations, action plan. 16 pages.

## What's surprising in the findings

The biggest opportunity was not top-line growth, but fulfillment efficiency: the lowest-margin SKUs were causing the largest stockouts and worst delivery times. Rebalancing inventory by channel improved working capital without sacrificing service levels.
